Complete Guide to the National Postal Museum

The Smithsonian's National Postal Museum stands as America's premier institution dedicated to preserving and presenting the rich history of mail communication. Located just steps from Union Station in Washington D.C., this often-overlooked gem offers visitors an immersive journey through centuries of postal innovation, cultural impact, and technological advancement.

Historical Background

The museum's origins trace back to the Smithsonian's long-standing interest in postal history:

Key Milestones:
  • 1886: Smithsonian receives its first philatelic donation
  • 1970s: National Philatelic Collection established
  • 1990: USPS and Smithsonian agree to create museum
  • July 30, 1993: Museum officially opens to public
  • 2013: Major renovation completed

Architectural Significance

The museum occupies the historic City Post Office Building:

  • Designed by Daniel Burnham's firm (completed by Ernest Graham)
  • Classical Revival architecture with Beaux-Arts influences
  • Original construction cost: $3 million (about $85 million today)
  • 200,000 square feet of space (museum occupies 75,000 sq ft)
  • Notable features: 90-foot ceiling in atrium, marble floors, ornate detailing

Permanent Exhibits

The museum's core exhibitions explore diverse aspects of postal history:

Systems at Work

This interactive exhibit demonstrates how mail moves from sender to recipient:

  • Historic mail sorting equipment and processes
  • Working optical character recognition machines
  • Hands-on package sorting challenges
  • Evolution of ZIP codes and addressing systems

Binding the Nation

Chronicles how mail service helped build American unity:

  • Pony Express and Overland Mail displays
  • Railway mail service artifacts
  • Rural Free Delivery implementation
  • Impact on westward expansion

Customers and Communities

Explores the human side of postal history:

  • Historic post office interiors recreated
  • Personal stories of postal workers
  • Role in civil rights movement
  • Disaster response and humanitarian efforts

The National Philatelic Collection

As the world's largest collection of stamps and philatelic materials:

  • Over 6 million items in collection
  • Includes rare 1847 U.S. postage stamps
  • Inverted Jenny plane error stamps
  • Comprehensive collection of U.S. and international stamps
  • Rotating displays highlight different themes

Notable Artifacts

The museum houses extraordinary pieces of postal history:

Artifact Description Significance
Owney the Postal Dog Stuffed remains of famous mail mascot Beloved 19th century railway mail dog
Wisconsin Pony Express Letter 1851 letter carried by private mail service Pre-dates official Pony Express
Hindenburg Crash Mail Surviving mail from 1937 disaster Rare aerophilatelic artifact
Civil War "Soldier's Mail" Collection of wartime correspondence Documents personal experiences

Transportation Collection

The museum's atrium features an impressive array of mail vehicles:

  • 1931 Ford Model A mail truck: Standard urban delivery vehicle
  • 1913 mail wagon: Horse-drawn predecessor
  • De Havilland DH-4 airplane: Early airmail carrier
  • Railway mail car: Complete sorting facility on rails
  • 1980s Long Life Vehicle: Iconic USPS truck
